Most Cited Work

How bodies matter: five themes for interaction design

Scott R. Klemmer, Björn Hartmann, and Leila Takayama. 2006
Cited 552 times In Proceedings of the 6th conference on Designing Interactive systems (DIS '06). ACM, New York, NY, USA, 140-149.
Physical interaction with interfaces is crucial are are often overlooked
Several advantages of body movement for interface interaction
Five Themes:
1. Thinking through doing - how mind and body are deeply interconnected - How Physical interaction with the world - cognitive dev. during childhood - Use of gestures - better communication - Example : Programmers hand stuck to keyboards are likely to hinder their thoughts
2. Performance: - Bodies are capable of performing complex actions - Eg : Surgeon. Such actions are rarely used in interfaces - Motor memory - How some guis employ same actions - better muscle memory 3. Visibility - Role of artifcats in collaboration - Sitauted Learning - watching someone interact with a system can Help
4. Risk - interfaces tend to reduce risk to very low - Retaining risks and uncertainity in interfaces can help - People know actions can lead to dangers. Better prepared and committed. - Flight simulators
5. Thick practice - Real world modelling of interfaces can Help - DJ system using turntables and vinyl records Theory Used: - Embodied Cognition - Example given by Dr. Caine in last class - Touch interfaces vs TurnDials in cars
  • Embodied Cognition vs Human Cognition
  • Five themes
    • Thinking through doing
    • Performance
    • Visibility
    • Risk
    • Thickness of practice
Most Cited Work

ButterflyNet: a mobile capture and access system for field biology research

Ron Yeh, Chunyuan Liao, Scott Klemmer, et al. 2006
Cited 269 times Proceedings of the SIGCHI conference on Human Factors in computing systems
biology fieldwork generates a wealth of qualitative and quantitative information
. Biologists rely on their notebooks as the primary record of observations, plans, measurements, and result
ButterflyNet, a system that integrates paper notes with information explicitly captured and ambiently available in field sites: digital photographs, sensor network data, and GPS
Digital Pen, Camera and Paper Notebook
A data browser and a spreadsheet
A first-use study with 14 biologists found this system to offer richer capture with minimal overhead, in a manner felicitous with current practice.
Theory: External Cognition
  • Use of notebooks in field research
  • Interactive system with notebooks
  • Rich information capture with minimim overhead
  • Advanced Interaction Design

CritiqueKit: A Mixed-Initiative, Real-Time Interface For Improving Feedback

C. Ailie Fraser, Tricia J. Ngoon, Ariel S. Weingarten, Mira Dontcheva, and Scott Klemmer. 2017
No Citations In Adjunct Publication of the 30th Annual ACM Symposium on User Interface Software and Technology (UIST '17). ACM, New York, NY, USA, 7-9.

Feedbacks are important
SHORT INTRO TO PEER FEEDBACK
System to guide users to give better feedback

Juxtapeer: Comparative Peer Review Yields Higher Quality Feedback and Promotes Deeper Reflection

Julia Cambre, Scott Klemmer, and Chinmay Kulkarni. 2018
1 Citation Special Award In Proceedings of the 2018 CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems (CHI '18). ACM, New York, NY, USA, Paper 294, 13 pages
A rails app
THEORY - Apply contrasting cases - peer review
Instead of reviewing assignments seperately, review an assignment and compare it with another
Two Sets - learners who used this and normal
